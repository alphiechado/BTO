Lifestyle: Top Five Nigerian Podcasts

1. The Daily Vulnerable

This is a self-improvement podcast that helps listeners work through personal challenges and emotional growth.

2. TechCabal Weekly

A podcast with technology news and analysis that focuses on the Tech sector in Africa every week.

3. I Said What I Said (ISWIS)
Hosted by Jola Ayeye and Feyikemi Abudu

Gist is the compositional hub of this podcast where no subject is off-limits.

4. Disunomics
Hosted by Disu Fasasi

This podcast simplifies complex subjects like economics, finance, and business so the average person can understand them.

5. A Music in Time
Hosted by Osagie Alonge

This podcast explores in detail how Nigerian music has changed since the late 1990s.

Being the Brand

The ipNX Brand in Action Tips

Being the brand is not about doing extra work, it is about doing regular things the ipNX way.

Be Customer-Centric

Ask one extra question to better understand a customer's need before responding.

Show Integrity

Own your mistakes early. Transparency builds trust.

Be Innovative

Think of one task you can simplify this week and suggest a change.

Small actions, big culture.

Real Talk: What Would You Do?

You get this message from a customer:

"I have complained about this before. This is the third time. Are you even serious?"

You have fixed the issue. But... how do you respond?
Apologize and move on?
Explain why it happened (again)?
Offer a little something extra?
Stay silent and let the fix speak?

Think like ipNX. Which value or personality trait would guide your reply?

We'll feature a few interesting takes in the next edition.

No one is watching. Being the Brand means living out our values in the work we do and the way we treat each other.

Whether it is showing respect in a tough conversation, offering solutions with creativity, or holding ourselves accountable, every small action shapes the ipNX experience.

Let us keep bringing the brand to life. One moment at a time.
